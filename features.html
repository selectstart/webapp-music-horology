02 — Grand Seiko–style Regulator

Tri-Synchro Glide Seconds

Inspired by the Grand Seiko Spring Drive's glide-spring regulator. The centre seconds wheel (R78) meshes the escape pinion (R14) at a strict 78:14 ratio — so the hand is always geared, not cosmetic, and it stops when the music stops. A viscous exponential follower with τ = 0.35 s smooths the stepped angle, producing the silky sweep that defines a Spring Drive without breaking mechanical honesty.

⚙
Strict gearing
The hand is driven by the escape wheel via a 78:14 ratio. It speeds up, slows down, and stops with the tempo — never animating independently.
◉
Glide flywheel
A small R12 flywheel on the seconds rim spins at 6.5× the centre wheel — the tri-synchro analogue spinning visibly at the pivot, flanked by two magnetic pole shoes and a copper coil winding.
∿
Exponential follower
csDeg += (strict − csDeg) × (1 − e^(−dt / 0.35)). The hand glides between ticks while always tracking the exact gear ratio on average.
03 — Audio Intelligence

Two Input Modes

File mode analyses the whole song before playback begins: BPM detection, offline section segmentation, and section-by-section energy classification drive the complications. Mic mode runs in real time with no lookahead — a Bark-band flux onset detector feeds a PLL-locked tempo tracker that re-estimates every 0.5 seconds.

♫
File mode
Drop mp3 / wav / m4a. BPM is detected via autocorrelation with an octave-preference fix (no half-tempo locking on fast songs). The escape wheel's beat-grid servo locks ticks to the actual song beats.
◎
Mic mode
Live tempo tracking: AGC / echo-cancel disabled, Bark-band flux with rolling mean+σ onset threshold, PLL phase lock. Locks in ≈ 3 s, re-locks to a new tempo in ≈ 4 s.
⊗
Section detection
Own radix-2 FFT, 12 log-spaced bands, cosine-distance novelty curve, peak-picked boundaries. Segments classified by energy z-score: chorus / verse / quiet. Complications engage on the upbeat.
∿
Rolling normalisation
RMS, bass, and spectral centroid are all normalised against their own decaying peak — not absolute levels — so quiet masters and loud masters both drive the full range of movement.
04 — Horological Complications

Four Complications

Each complication is mechanical in behaviour: springs throw levers, discs snap into windows, hands flyback rather than fade. They engage and disengage as the song's structure changes — not on timers, but on musical events.

⟳
Flyback chronograph
Chorus sections engage the column-wheel lever. A 30-minute subdial totaliser accumulates elapsed time; at disengage the lever flies back and the hand springs to zero.
◐
Moonphase
Rises into its aperture on quiet / bridge sections. The gold moon disc self-rotates on a 90-second period and drifts by its drive wheel's gear ratio. Stars remain fixed in the aperture.
▷
Power reserve
Bass energy charges the fan indicator in real time. At silence the reserve collapses at 10× the passive drain rate — the movement visibly losing power as the room goes quiet.
31
Big date
Snaps forward at each detected section boundary. Twin digit discs are clipped to individual windows with gold bezels. Only the disc that changed numerically snaps — the tens digit is stable through most of a song.
05 — Materials & Lighting

Reactive Light & Material

Move your mouse over the movement to reposition the bench lamp. All metal surfaces — bridges, wheels, hands, jewels, the glide flywheel — respond via shared SVG gradient definitions rather than per-element filters, keeping animation at 60 fps regardless of scene complexity.

◈
Pointer-following lamp
A 0.28 s inertia exponential follower tracks the cursor. Light aim, specular glints, and cast shadows all update each frame. The drop shadow falls away from the light source at every position.
◑
Spectral material shift
Bright, treble-heavy passages shift the train toward cool rhodium (#dfe2e6 → #84888f). Bass-heavy passages warm it to rose gold (#e9c08e → #a4724b). The shift takes ≈ 2.5 s to reach a new centroid — metal, not a VU meter.
◇
Jewel cap reflections
Each of the 30 jewels carries a domed cap gradient re-aimed toward the light source. Pallet stones, balance jewels, and cap stones each have distinct ruby-red / steel-dome profiles.
〇
Exhibition skeleton
The plate is skeletonised: all four gear wheels, the keyless cascade (ratchet → crown → winding → setting), coupling wheel, and moon-drive wheel are fully visible and rotating with correct direction and ratio.
